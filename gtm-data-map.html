The GTM Data Map

“But our data
is a mess.”
Good. Let’s map it.

Every revenue team says it. Almost none of them can actually see what they have. Before you fix anything — before you automate, forecast, or feed a single model — you need one clear picture of every GTM signal at your disposal. That picture is the GTM Data Map.

Get your GTM Data Map See what’s on the map
The Real Problem

The mess isn’t the problem.
Not knowing what’s in it is.

Messy data doesn’t lose deals. Invisible data does. When you can’t see what you’re sitting on, you can’t tell the goldmine from the noise from the liability — so it all just sits there.

01

Signal you never use

Calls, emails, and notes full of buyer intent that never reach a decision. The answer was in the data. Nobody could find it.

02

Three systems, three answers

The CRM says one thing, the call platform another, the spreadsheet a third. No source of truth means no trust.

03

Data you can’t rely on

Stale fields. Blank stages. Close reasons picked at random. A pipeline that quietly lies to you every Monday.

04

No idea what’s usable

You want to put this data to work — automation, forecasting, AI. You just don’t know which parts are ready and which are landmines.

What We Map

Every source of GTM signal you have.

We inventory the data your go-to-market motion generates — the obvious systems and the ones hiding in inboxes and call libraries — then categorize each source and rate it for coverage, quality, and readiness.

01

Conversations

Call recordings, demos, and discovery notes — your Gong, Chorus, or Fireflies library.

02

CRM & Pipeline

Accounts, opportunities, stages, activities, and close reasons — the system of record.

03

Marketing & Demand

Campaigns, attribution, web analytics, MQL flow, and ad-platform data.

04

Product & Usage

Telemetry, activation, adoption, and the product-qualified signals buyers send.

05

Customer & Retention

Support tickets, CS notes, NPS, and the early signals of churn and expansion.

06

Buyer & Market

Win/loss records, competitive intel, review sites, intent data, and enrichment.

07

Outbound & Engagement

Sequences, email opens and replies, LinkedIn touches, and reply threads.

08

Revenue & Finance

ARR, cohorts, forecast, quota, and comp — where GTM meets the P&L.

How DARK MATTER Works

Intelligence as a service.
Not another platform.

DARK MATTER is a technology-enabled consultancy. We run proprietary language models on private infrastructure, analyze your competitive deals at scale, and deliver finished intelligence to your inbox. There's nothing to log into, nothing to learn, and nothing to maintain.

Let's clear something up.

DARK MATTER is not

A SaaS platform

There's no login. No dashboard. No seat licenses. No implementation project. No training sessions for your team. No "adoption" problem to solve.

A CI tool you need to manage

You don't configure it, maintain it, or worry about whether your team is using it correctly. We handle all of that. Your team receives finished intelligence, not raw data.

A consulting firm that bills by the hour

We don't send junior analysts to shadow your reps for six months and produce a deck. Our language models analyze every call at scale. The output is synthesized by senior practitioners, not interns.

A replacement for your team

We don't compete with your PMMs, enablement leads, or RevOps team. We do the analysis they don't have time to do, and deliver it in a form that makes them more effective.

DARK MATTER is

A technology-enabled intelligence service

Proprietary AI models handle the scale. Senior practitioners handle the synthesis. You get the output — finished, actionable intelligence delivered on a predictable cadence.

An extension of your revenue team

Think of us as the competitive intelligence department you'd need 3–5 full-time hires to build — deployed in two weeks, producing artifacts from day one, improving every quarter.

A service with zero adoption friction

Battlecards arrive by email. Competitive radar lands in Slack. Board narratives come as finished documents. Your team doesn't learn a new tool — they open their inbox.

Built on infrastructure we own and control

Our language models run on private infrastructure. Your call data never passes through third-party AI APIs. No OpenAI. No shared models. No training on your data. Full control.

How the technology works.

Every engagement follows the same three-layer process. The AI handles the scale. The practitioners handle the judgment. The output is intelligence your team can act on immediately.

01

Ingest

We connect read-only to your call recording platform and CRM. Our models process every competitive conversation from the last two quarters — transcripts, metadata, deal outcomes, buyer sentiment. Not a sample. Every call.

02

Analyze

Proprietary language models extract competitive patterns at a scale no human team can match. Objection frequency, talk track effectiveness, pricing sensitivity, competitor mention clustering, win/loss correlation. The models identify what matters. Senior analysts validate what's real.

03

Deliver

Raw analysis becomes finished intelligence: board-ready narratives, living battlecards, competitive radar, threat assessments. Written for a leadership audience by practitioners who've held the CRO seat. Delivered on your schedule, in the formats your team already uses.

Your data never leaves our control.

Competitive sales conversations are some of the most sensitive data in your organization. We treat them that way. Our security posture is built for enterprise procurement — because that's who we serve.

Private language models on private infrastructure

Our AI models run on infrastructure we own and operate. Your call data is never sent to OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, or any third-party API for processing. No shared models. No co-mingling. No exceptions.

Read-only access, always

We connect to your systems with the minimum permissions necessary. Read-only access to call recordings. Read-only access to CRM. We never modify, delete, or export your raw data. Our outputs are synthesized intelligence artifacts — not copies of your recordings.

Your data never trains our models

Your competitive intelligence stays yours. We never use client data to train, fine-tune, or improve our models for other clients. Your patterns, your insights, your competitive advantages are never shared, blended, or generalized across our client base.

NDA and security review from day one

We sign your NDA before we see a single call. We're happy to complete your security questionnaire, work within your vendor management process, and accommodate any additional controls your InfoSec team requires. We've done this before.

Data retention you control

You decide how long we retain processed data. At the end of any engagement, we provide a complete data deletion certification. Your intelligence artifacts are yours to keep. Our working data is purged on your timeline.

Encrypted at every layer

Data encrypted in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest (AES-256). Access controls enforce least-privilege across our entire team. Audit logs available on request. We operate like the data is ours — because trust is the foundation of what we do.

Questions we hear. Answers we give.

"We already have Gong. Why do we need this?"

Gong is excellent at recording and transcribing calls. It's a capture layer. DARK MATTER is a synthesis layer. Gong can tell you what was said on a call. We can tell you what 500 calls mean for your competitive strategy. We start where Gong's built-in analytics end — cross-referencing call data with CRM outcomes, identifying patterns across your entire deal population, and producing strategic artifacts no recording platform can generate.

"Can't we just use ChatGPT for this?"

You could send individual transcripts to ChatGPT and get call summaries. That's not what we do. We analyze 500+ calls as a corpus, cross-reference with CRM deal data, identify statistical patterns, validate them against outcomes, and synthesize the results into strategic intelligence written for a board audience. That requires purpose-built models, domain expertise, and a practitioner who understands what a CRO actually needs. ChatGPT gives you summaries. We give you strategy.

"We already have a competitive intelligence tool."

Most CI tools — Klue, Crayon, Kompyte — aggregate publicly available competitor information: press releases, job postings, G2 reviews. That's market intelligence. It's useful, but it doesn't tell you what's happening inside your deals. DARK MATTER analyzes what buyers actually say about competitors when they're on the phone with your reps. That's a fundamentally different data source, and it produces a fundamentally different kind of intelligence.

"How do I know the AI is accurate?"

You don't have to trust the AI. Every pattern our models surface is validated by senior practitioners before it reaches you. If a model says "pricing is the primary loss driver against Competitor X," we verify it against deal outcomes, talk track analysis, and CRM data before we include it in your intelligence. The AI handles scale. Humans handle judgment. You get both.

"My team won't adopt another tool."

Good news: there's nothing to adopt. Battlecards arrive as documents in email. Competitive radar lands in Slack. Board narratives are finished decks. We deliver intelligence in the channels your team already lives in. The adoption rate for "read your email" is 100%.

"We tried this before and it didn't work."

Most competitive intelligence efforts fail because they depend on manual process that doesn't scale — one person trying to listen to calls, a PMM who gets pulled into launches, a quarterly review that keeps slipping. We don't depend on your team's bandwidth. Our models run continuously. The intelligence compounds whether your team is busy or not. That's the difference between a process and a system.

"This sounds expensive."

The Intelligence Audit is $7,500 — less than a single month of most CI tools. Ongoing retainers start at $25,000 per quarter — less than a junior analyst's fully loaded cost. And unlike a junior analyst, DARK MATTER processes every call, every week, from day one. If 15–20% of your winnable pipeline is leaking to fixable competitive gaps, the math isn't close.

"What if we want to bring this capability in-house?"

We'd be happy to help you do that eventually. Some clients use DARK MATTER for 12–18 months while they build internal capability, then transition to a lighter advisory relationship. We're not trying to create dependency. We're trying to prove that competitive intelligence, done properly, changes your win rate. How you choose to sustain it long-term is your call.

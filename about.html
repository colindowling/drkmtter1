01
The Origin

When I was younger I played a lot of poker. At casinos, with friends, online. And I learned a universal truth most poker players understand: I can't remember the biggest pots I ever won, but I remember with excruciating detail every bad beat I ever suffered.

In the last 25 years of B2B selling, I've won some incredible deals — including GoDaddy's largest-ever enterprise contract, WP Engine's first five-figure monthly hosting contract, and Alembic's first true enterprise deal. And still, the details I remember most clearly are the deals I didn't win. The ones where I felt blindsided at the finish line. Where I had the verbal approval for an order form that never came. The ones where I later learned a deal I had committed to the board had gone to a competitor.

Sell for long enough and your own "bad beats" start to stack up. If you've made it this far, they probably already have.

02
The Belief

I don't believe the best product wins. I believe the best salesperson does. And when I lost a deal I took it personally — the other person with a seat at the table outsold me.

The better salesperson won.

Each time I wondered: what could I have done differently? What should I have known? What could I have known? How could I better stack the deck in my favor for the next deal, and the next, and the next...

DARK MATTER exists to give B2B sales leaders and their teams the exact intelligence they need to win more deals.
